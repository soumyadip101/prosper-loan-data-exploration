Loan Characteristics affecting Borrower's APR from Prosper Loan Data¶

by Soumyadip Mitra¶

Investigation Overview¶

In this investigation, I wanted to look at the characteristics of loans that could be used to predict their borrower APR. After exploring different characteristics, I restricted my focus on the following loan characteristics:-

  • Original loan amount
  • Borrower's Prosper rating
  • Loan term
  • Borrower's stated monthly income.

Dataset Overview¶

The dataset consisted of 81 attributes of 113,937 loans. The attributes included Loan original amount, Borrower's Prosper rating, Loan term, Borrower's stated monthly income, as well as many other features such as borrower's employment status, debt to income ratio, current loan status etc. Out of 81 attributes a total of 9 attributes were choosen for analysis of which finally I settled for 6 attributes for explanatory purposes. 352 data points were removed due to very large stated monthly income which seemed as outliers and 25 missing borrower APR was replaced by imputing APR as BorrowerAPR= BorrowerRate + fee, where fee = BorrowerAPR - BorrowerRate. 8491 missing DebtToIncome Ratio was replaced with 0.0 value and the rest of the remaining missing values were left as Null.

Prosper Rating Effect on Relationship between APR and Loan Amount¶

The borrower APR decreases with better rating whereas the loan amount increases with better rating. Interestingly, the relationship between Borrower APR and Loan amount turns from negative to slightly positive when the Prosper ratings are increased from HR to A or better. This may be because people with A or AA ratings tend to borrow more money and increasing APR could prevent them from borrowing even more and maximize the profit. But people with lower ratings tend to borrow less money, hence decreasing APR for higher loan amounts for them could encourage them to borrow more.
